Cookie Policy

Last updated: June 2026 · PawsOut Skin Care Treatments (“we”, “our”, “us”) uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website. This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, how we use them, and your choices regarding their use. By using our website, you consent to the use of cookies in accordance with this policy, unless you have set your browser to refuse them.

1. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, tablet, or smartphone) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, enhance user experience, and provide information to the site owners. Cookies can be “persistent” or “session” cookies: persistent cookies remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them, while session cookies are deleted when you close your browser.

2. How we use cookies

PawsOut uses cookies for the following purposes:

  • Strictly necessary: These cookies are essential for the website to function properly. They enable you to navigate our site and use its features, such as accessing secure areas and booking appointments. Without these cookies, we cannot provide the requested services.
  • Performance and analytics: We use cookies to understand how visitors interact with our website, which pages are most popular, and how we can improve the user experience. This helps us optimise our content and design.
  • Functionality: These cookies remember your preferences (such as language or region) and provide enhanced, more personalised features. They may also be used to remember changes you have made to text size or other customisable parts of the site.
  • Targeting and advertising: We may use cookies to deliver relevant advertisements and measure the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns. These cookies track your browsing habits and may be used to limit the number of times you see an advert.

3. Types of cookies we use

We use both first‑party and third‑party cookies. First‑party cookies are set by us and are used for functionality and analytics. Third‑party cookies are set by external services we use, such as Google Analytics, payment gateways, and social media platforms. These third parties may combine the information collected with other data they have about you.

3.1 Specific cookies

  • _ga (Google Analytics): used to distinguish users and track session data. Expires after 2 years.
  • _gid (Google Analytics): used to distinguish users. Expires after 24 hours.
  • cookie_consent: stores your preference regarding cookie usage. Expires after 1 year.
  • session_id: manages your session and login state. Expires when you close your browser.

4. Your cookie choices

You have the right to decide whether to accept or reject cookies. You can exercise your cookie preferences by using the consent dialog at the bottom of this page, or by adjusting your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings, which may include:

  • Viewing and deleting specific cookies.
  • Blocking third‑party cookies.
  • Blocking all cookies from specific websites.
  • Blocking all cookies altogether.

Please note that if you choose to block or delete cookies, some parts of our website may not function properly, and you may not be able to access certain features, such as booking appointments.

6. Third‑party cookies and services

We use third‑party services that may place cookies on your device. These include:

  • Google Analytics: We use Google Analytics to collect information about how visitors use our site. This helps us improve our website and services. Google’s privacy policy is available at https://policies.google.com/privacy.
  • Social media plugins: Our website includes social media sharing buttons (e.g., Facebook, Instagram) that may set cookies to track your interaction with these platforms.
  • Payment processors: When you book a treatment, our payment gateway may set cookies to facilitate secure transactions.

7. Cookie retention

The retention period for cookies varies depending on their purpose. Session cookies are deleted when you close your browser, while persistent cookies remain on your device for a set period (ranging from 24 hours to 2 years). You can manually delete cookies at any time through your browser settings.

8. Consent and control

When you first visit our website, we display a cookie consent dialog that allows you to accept or decline non‑essential cookies. You can change your preference at any time by clicking the “Cookie Settings” link in our footer or by using the consent dialog below. If you decline cookies, we will only set strictly necessary cookies to ensure basic functionality.

9. Legal basis for using cookies

For cookies that are not strictly necessary, we rely on your consent as the legal basis for processing. You have the right to withdraw your consent at any time. For strictly necessary cookies, we rely on our legitimate interest in providing a functional and secure website.
